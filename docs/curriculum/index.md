# Are-Self in the Classroom — A 4th Grade Teacher's Curriculum Guide

**A six-week journey where students learn WITH an AI brain — not about one.**

This curriculum puts Are-Self into the hands of 4th graders across every core subject. Students don't just use AI — they build personas, design experiments, form memories, and ask one more question. By the end, they'll have a working understanding of how AI reasons, and more importantly, they'll have practiced the habits of mind that make any tool powerful: inclusion, humility, and inquiry.

## Who This Is For

You're a 4th grade teacher. You might be technical, you might not be. You might have one classroom computer or a lab full of Chromebooks with a server in the closet. This curriculum works either way, because Are-Self was built for exactly this scenario — a kid with whatever hardware is available.

You don't need to be an AI expert. You need to be curious. The students will meet you there.

## The Three Scipraxian Habits

Every week weaves three habits into the activities. These aren't AI skills — they're human skills. Are-Self just gives students a reason to practice them:

- **Inclusion** — Who got left outside the circle? Go find them.
- **Humility** — "I don't know yet" is the beginning of every good thing.
- **Inquiry** — After every answer, ask one more question.

These come from the companion storybook *Mira and the Are-Self* (Book One of the Scipraxian Tales). Reading chapters aloud is optional but powerful — the story gives students a character who models the habits before they practice them.

## What Students Will Be Able to Do

By the end of this unit, students will:

1. **Explain** what an AI reasoning engine does, using the brain region analogy
2. **Create** an AI Identity with a purpose, personality, and tools
3. **Collaborate** with AI as a thinking partner across all core subjects
4. **Evaluate** AI-generated content for accuracy, bias, and usefulness
5. **Apply** the three scipraxian habits (inclusion, humility, inquiry) to real problems
6. **Present** an original project that demonstrates meaningful AI collaboration
7. **Articulate** the difference between AI as a tool and AI as a replacement for thinking

## Common Core Alignment

This curriculum addresses the following 4th grade Common Core clusters:

- **ELA:** W.4.1–W.4.3 (opinion, informative, narrative writing), W.4.6 (technology for writing), SL.4.1 (collaborative discussions), SL.4.4 (presentations)
- **Math:** 4.OA (operations and algebraic thinking), 4.MD (measurement and data), MP.1 (make sense of problems), MP.3 (construct arguments)
- **Science (NGSS):** 4-PS4 (waves), 4-ESS (earth systems), SEP-1 (asking questions), SEP-3 (planning investigations), SEP-4 (analyzing data)
- **Social Studies (C3):** D1.1–D1.5 (developing questions), D2.Civ (civic life), D3.1 (gathering sources), D4.1–D4.8 (communicating conclusions)

## Hardware & Time Requirements

- **Minimum hardware:** One computer running Are-Self, projected for the class. Students rotate in pairs.
- **Ideal hardware:** A computer lab or 1:1 devices connected to an Are-Self server on the local network.
- **Time per day:** 45–60 minutes (fits a standard instructional block)
- **Total time:** 30 instructional days across 6 weeks

## A Note on the Storybook

*Mira and the Are-Self* is a companion text written for this age group. Each week's opening activity references a chapter. You can read it aloud (10–15 minutes), assign it as independent reading, or skip it entirely — the curriculum works without it. But if you read it, the habits land harder, because students see a kid their age practicing them before they do.

The full text will be available at are-self.com/docs/storybook (coming soon) and as a printable PDF.
